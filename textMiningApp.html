Text Mining Website

An interactive website that allows you to easily extract and visualize data from a provided text by using different text analyis methods.

The Website

While working on my summer research project, which involved analyzing text from town hall meetings, I became very interested in text mining. I started analyzing other texts that were interesting to me, such as books, movie scripts, and emails.

With this website, I aim to make available a tool to easily gather and visualize different aspects of your text data using various text analysis methods, such as Sentiment analysis, word counts, and TF-IDF analysis.

The website also provides a simple explanation of each to increase accessibility.

  • Check out the website

Sentiment Analyisis

The website features a sentiment analysis tab, where users can:

  • See how positive and negative their text is by the words it uses.
  • Identify the words that most contribute to the sentiment of their texts
  • Determine how the sentiment changes throughout the text.

    • It also allows users to select between 4 different lexicons, Afinn, Bing, Loughran, and NRC, to analyze their text. Each lexicon has different uses, so users can choose the one that best fits their text.

    Word Counts

    The word counts tab, as the name suggests, allows users to see the frequency of words in their text.It includes:

  • A graph showing the most frequent words and their coununts.
  • A graph showing the most fequent Bigrams(two consequitive words)
  • A word map, showing the relationship- what words often preeced or succed others- between the most common words in the text

    • TF-IDF

    This tab calculates the TF-IDF of each word in a text by comparing it to the frequency of the word in otheer documents. It allows users to see the most important words in their text when compared to other texts. It includes:

  • A graph showing the words that stand out from each text
  • A graph showing the word that are the most common across all texts
  • File upload options to compare a maximum of 4 texts
