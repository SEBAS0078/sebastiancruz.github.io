Vermont's Meeting Minutes

A text analysis exploring how Vermont's Selectboard meeting minutes reflect local governance and community issues

Purpose

With 247 municipalities, each of these with a Selectboard that meets around twice per month, Vermont produces more than 5000 Selectboard meeting minutes per year.

These written records contain information on current topics and issues the towns face, so they are an optimal source for gathering data on towns circumstances over time. In this summer project, I explored Vermont’s selectboard meeting minutes using a variety of text analysis methods such as sentiment analysis, word couunts, and TF-IDF to study town governance and identify patterns or insights.

To wrap-up the project, I built and published an interactive R shiny web app to function as a public, searchable database for the dataset we collected.

  • Check out the website

The Dataset

The dataset used for the project was collected in collaboration with other students in my Data Science class. I refined and expanded it over the course of the project.

It contains 5,329 Selectboard meeting minutes from 204 Vermont towns in 2023. This is 80% of all Vermont municipalities.

For Middlebury, an expanded dataset adds four more years of minutes and one year of meeting transcripts taken from Youtube uploads of meetings.

The Website

The interactive website offers three main features:

  • Filterable Dataset: Access and download the complete dataset.
  • Explore the Dataset: Analyze data using methods like Sentiment Analysis, TF-IDF, and word counts with visualizations (e.g., maps, timelines, and tables).
  • Middlebury Subset: Dive deeper into Middlebury’s dataset, comparing transcripts with minutes to assess accuracy and focus.
